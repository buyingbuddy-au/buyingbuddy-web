## Mission

BuyingBuddy helps everyday car buyers not get ripped off — through simple, affordable tools and practical guidance built by someone who spent 15 years on the other side of the deal.

---

## What Buying Buddy Is

Buying Buddy is a low-cost, self-serve toolkit for people buying cars privately in Australia (starting QLD). It exists because everyday buyers get burned on private car sales — they don't know what a PPSR is, they don't understand their legal obligations, they don't know if the price is fair, and they let sellers dictate the process.

The product is the tools. Not Jordan. Not a consulting service. Not a concierge. Jordan's 15+ years of dealership experience (Lexus, BMW, wholesale, retail, private sales) is baked INTO the product — the checklists, the contracts, the AI guidance, the step-by-step flows. The expertise is productised, not delivered live.

## What Buying Buddy Is NOT

- Not a buyer's agent or concierge service
- Not a marketplace or listing platform
- Not competing with Carsales, Facebook Marketplace, or dealer platforms
- Not a premium-priced product
- Not a full-time job for Jordan — this is designed to run with minimal daily involvement
- Not trying to be a big enterprise SaaS — small, profitable, and automated is the goal

## Parked Products

These have been deliberately removed or shelved. They may return later but are NOT part of the current build:

- **Ask Buddy (AI Chat — /buddy):** Standalone chatbot page. Removed. If AI guidance returns, it will be contextual — embedded inside tools at the moment it's useful (e.g. finance payoff walkthrough after a PPSR flags debt), not a separate destination page.
- **Buyer's Agent Service ($997):** Removed from all public-facing pages. May return as a hidden post-purchase upsell in a different form (see Hidden Upsell section). Not the business.

## Why It Exists

Jordan has sold hundreds of cars across dealerships and private sales over 15 years. The same patterns repeat constantly:

- Buyers don't know what a PPSR is or why it matters
- Buyers don't know they need a roadworthy (Safety Certificate) to register a car in QLD
- Buyers let sellers dictate payment terms on financed cars — handing $20K to a stranger instead of paying the lender directly, leaving themselves completely exposed
- Buyers don't do inspections, or do them after they're already emotionally committed
- Buyers don't have proper paperwork — no contract, no receipt, no condition report
- The "solutions" that exist (CarHistory, Carify, etc.) charge $40-60+ for glorified PPSR wrappers with valuations nobody asked for — monster margins on data that costs a fraction of what they charge

There's a gap: genuinely useful, affordable tools built by someone who actually knows how the game works.

## Competitive Position

"The other guys charge $40-60 for a PPSR wrapper with a valuation you didn't ask for. We charge $4.95 for the actual data, and the rest of the tools are free. Built by someone who sold cars for 15 years and knows exactly how buyers get ripped off."

Future opportunity: undercut the CarHistory/Carify model by offering equivalent or better value at a fraction of the price. They run on fat margins. We run lean and automated.

## Product Stack

### Free Tools (lead capture + trust builders)

| Tool | What It Does | Conversion Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Free Check** | Paste a listing URL or enter car details → get a quick sanity check on price, red flags, known issues | Email capture, entry point to funnel |
| **Inspection Checklist** | 14-point guided checklist to use standing next to the car. Full interactive version + printable version | Trust builder, brand awareness |
| **Contract Pack** | QLD private sale contract, receipt of payment, vehicle condition report, transfer of rego guide. Four documents. Proven — Jordan used these to sell his own car | Email-gated download, lead capture |

### Paid Products

| Product | Price | What It Does | Delivery |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PPSR Report** | $4.95 | Finance owing, stolen status, write-off check. Plain-English summary. Official PPSR data. | Automated (goal), manual with failsafe (current) |
| **Deal Pack** | $9.99 | PPSR + Deal Room access + Contract Pack bundled together. Everything you need to close a private sale properly. | Automated |

### The Deal Room

Shared digital workspace for buyer and seller to manage the handover process. Tracks steps, holds documents, provides structure to what is usually a chaotic process. Included in the $9.99 Deal Pack.

### Value-Add Content (built into tools, not sold separately)

This is where Jordan's expertise becomes the product. Contextual, specific guidance that appears at the right moment in the buying process:

- **Finance payoff guidance:** If a PPSR shows finance owing, the tool walks the buyer through exactly how to handle it safely — pay the lender directly, never hand cash to the seller to "pay it off themselves." Explains the legal exposure and what dealers do vs what civilians do.
- **Negotiation prompts:** Practical tips surfaced at relevant points — what to say, what leverage you have, how to read seller behaviour.
- **Red flag alerts:** Specific warnings based on make/model/year (known issues, recall history, common scams for that vehicle type).
- **Legal requirements:** QLD-specific — Safety Certificate requirements, transfer of registration process, what happens if you skip steps.

This content is what separates Buying Buddy from a generic PPSR wrapper. It's not just data — it's guidance from someone who's been on the other side of the table.

## Pricing Strategy

**Option A (current):** Two price points.

- $4.95 PPSR standalone — low-friction impulse buy. Wide open door.
- $9.99 Deal Pack — PPSR + Deal Room + Contracts bundled. Upsell in the flow: "About to buy this car? Get the full kit for $9.99."

The $4.95 stays accessible for people who just want a quick check. The $9.99 captures more value from people who are actively in a deal.

**Revenue model:** Volume-based, automated. No human involvement in delivery (goal state). Jordan's time is spent on marketing and product improvement, not fulfilment.

---

## Hidden Upsell — Post-Purchase Only

**$99 Quick Review** — 10-minute phone call or async review where a dealer-trained expert gives an opinion on a specific car before the buyer commits. Live dealer valuation perspective, negotiation advice, or "should I buy this?" gut check.

**Rules:**
- This NEVER appears on the homepage or any marketing page
- This ONLY appears after someone has purchased something (PPSR or Deal Pack)
- It's positioned as a subtle suggestion, not a hard sell: "Want a dealer's eye on this before you commit?"
- This is future-state and may evolve into a sourcing service, inspection booking, or buyer's advocate offering — but that's not today's product

## Funnel & User Journey

```
[Awareness] → Google / social / word of mouth
     ↓
[Free Check] → Paste listing, get sanity check → email capture
     ↓
[PPSR $4.95] → Finance / stolen / write-off check
     ↓
  ┌─────────────────────────────┐
  │ PPSR clean?                 │ PPSR has issues?
  │ → Upsell to Deal Pack $9.99│ → Step-by-step guidance
  │ → Inspection checklist      │   (e.g. finance payoff flow)
  │ → Contract pack             │ → Still upsell Deal Pack
  └─────────────────────────────┘
     ↓
[Deal Room] → Buyer + seller manage handover
     ↓
[Post-purchase] → Hidden $99 Quick Review suggestion
```

Every tool endpoint funnels forward. Free Check → PPSR → Deal Pack → Deal Room. The contract pack appears as a natural next step after PPSR, not as a standalone peer in a grid.

**Do immediately:**

### Phase 2 — Tighten The Funnel (NEXT — after Phase 1 is live)

- Free Check results page → upsell to PPSR

- PPSR with finance flagged → contextual finance payoff guidance (step-by-step)

**Goal:** Every tool endpoint funnels forward. Users naturally move through Free Check → PPSR → Deal Pack → Deal Room. No dead ends.

### Phase 3 — Automate & Grow (LATER — only after real traction)

- PPSR semi-automation (browser agent, approval queue)
- PPSR full automation (after 50+ successful runs)
- More contextual guidance content (negotiation tips, red flag alerts by make/model, QLD legal requirements)
- SEO content / blog / guides
- Consider direct PPSR API integration at volume
- Consider expanding the $99 upsell into sourcing, inspection booking, or buyer's advocate
- Consider other states if QLD is proven

**Goal:** Jordan spends <30 min/day. Revenue from volume. Business runs alongside car buying/selling.

## Design & Copy Principles

- **Direct, not desperate.** State what it is. Don't oversell.
- **Short copy.** If it takes 6 sections to explain, it's too complicated.
- **Trust through simplicity.** No accounts required, no spam, no hidden fees.
- **QLD-specific.** Don't pretend to be national. Own the niche.
- **Anti-corporate.** Plain English. No marketing fluff. The target audience is regular people, not enterprise buyers.
- **Jordan's credibility is background, not foreground.** It informs the product. It's not the product.

---

## Anti-Drift Rules

These exist to prevent AI (or anyone) from pushing the product in directions Jordan hasn't approved:

1. **No adding services that require Jordan's live time** without explicit approval
2. **No premium pricing tiers** — the business model is low-cost, high-volume
3. **No buyer's agent / concierge positioning** on any public-facing page
4. **No feature creep** — every new feature must tie back to the funnel above
5. **No corporate copy** — if it sounds like a SaaS landing page template, rewrite it
6. **No national expansion language** — we're QLD until Jordan says otherwise
7. **The $99 upsell stays hidden** — post-purchase only, no exceptions
8. **When in doubt, keep it simple** — one clear thing done well beats five half-built features
9. **No standalone AI chat page** — AI guidance is contextual (embedded in tools), not a destination
10. **Roll back copy, not product** — when the site drifts, fix the messaging layer. Don't rebuild what's working.
