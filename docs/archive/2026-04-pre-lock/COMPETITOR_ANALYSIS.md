## Competitor Breakdown

### 1. Lemon Squad
**Model:** Mobile pre-purchase inspection (PPI) service. Mechanic comes to the car.  
**Pricing:** ~$295–$395 per inspection  
**What they do:** Assess the mechanical condition of a car the buyer has already found.  

**Weaknesses:**
- No buyer advocacy — they inspect, they don't advise on whether you should buy
- Buyer still has to find the car themselves (often the hardest part)
- No negotiation — buyer pays whatever the seller asks after getting the report
- Report quality varies by mechanic — inconsistent standards
- No industry pricing knowledge — can't tell you if you're paying retail on a wholesale car
- Reactive, not proactive — you bring them the car, they don't help you find a better one

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
Jordan finds the car, vets it at wholesale level before you even book an inspection, then uses the PPI to negotiate harder. The inspection isn't the end — it's a tool in the deal. That's a completely different service.

---

### 2. RACQ
**Model:** Automotive club offering PPI as a member benefit/service.  
**Pricing:** $219 (members) / $275 (non-members)  
**What they do:** Vehicle inspection service — mechanical condition check only.  

**Weaknesses:**
- Still no negotiation help — RACQ gives you the report and wishes you luck
- No car sourcing — buyer finds the car, RACQ just checks it
- RACQ inspectors are generalists — not wholesale/retail pricing experts
- Slow booking times in regional areas
- No accountability for the overall deal outcome — just the inspection
- Membership required to get the better rate (extra friction and cost)
- Perceived as bureaucratic and slow — not the punchy, personal service a private buyer needs

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
RACQ is a club benefit, not a buyer's service. They inspect. Jordan advocates. Big difference. Jordan will tell you "walk away from this one" or "I can get this for $2K less" — RACQ won't.

---

### 3. AutoGuru
**Model:** Lead generation marketplace connecting car owners to mechanics.  
**Pricing:** Free for consumers; mechanics pay per lead  
**What they do:** Help people book servicing and repairs. Not a buyer advocacy service.  

**Weaknesses:**
- **Doesn't help car buyers at all** — AutoGuru is for people who already own a car and need a mechanic
- No purchasing advice, no sourcing, no negotiation
- Mechanics are the customer — buyers are just the traffic
- Conflict of interest: mechanics pay to appear, so platform has no incentive to recommend the best one
- No Brisbane-specific buyer advice
- Zero industry pricing expertise for buyers

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
They're not even in the same game. AutoGuru is about car maintenance. BuyingBuddy is about not getting ripped off buying one. Different problem entirely. But buyers searching for "car inspection Brisbane" might land on AutoGuru — that's a keyword gap worth owning.

---

### 4. Airtasker PPI Brisbane
**Model:** Gig economy marketplace — post a task, mechanics bid on it.  
**Pricing:** Avg $150–$215 per inspection  
**What they do:** Cheap PPI via whoever bids lowest on the platform.  

**Weaknesses:**
- **Race to the bottom pricing** — lowest bid wins, which means least qualified mechanic often wins
- No consistency — different mechanic every time, different standards every time
- No buyer advocacy — they look at the car, write a report, that's it
- No negotiation, no sourcing, no relationship
- Liability is murky — if the report misses something, good luck recovering anything
- Buyers think they're getting a deal at $170 — they're getting what they pay for
- No specialist knowledge of used car market pricing

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
An Airtasker inspector might save you $150 on the inspection and cost you $3,000 on the deal. Jordan's $997 flat fee is the total cost of the service — and he's negotiating that fee back into the purchase price. It's not a cost, it's an investment.

---

### 5. Private Fleet
**Model:** New car broker/buying service.  
**Pricing:** Typically included in the deal via dealer kickback or flat fee ~$500–$990  
**What they do:** Negotiate new car deals on behalf of buyers. Fleet pricing leverage.  

**Weaknesses:**
- **New cars only** — zero help for used car buyers (the riskiest, most complex purchase)
- Broker makes money from dealer relationships — potential conflict of interest
- No mechanical knowledge or inspection coordination
- No help avoiding dodgy private sellers or auction cars with hidden history
- Sydney/Melbourne-centric — Brisbane coverage is thin
- Used car market is where buyers get burned; new cars have transparent pricing

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
Private Fleet handles the easy end of the market — new car pricing is relatively transparent, there's warranty protection, and the broker risk is low. Used cars are where people get properly ripped off. That's Jordan's lane. Different problem, completely different expertise required.

---

### 6. hipages
**Model:** Lead generation marketplace for tradies and service businesses.  
**Pricing:** Free for consumers; tradespeople pay per lead  
**What they do:** Connect homeowners and consumers with local tradespeople. Car inspections sometimes listed.  

**Weaknesses:**
- **Not built for car buyers** — automotive is a tiny slice of a home-services marketplace
- No buyer advocacy — mechanics are the product, not the advice
- Pay-per-lead model means mechanics focus on volume, not quality
- Zero car buying expertise — they're plumbers and electricians with an automotive category bolted on
- No pricing knowledge, no negotiation, no sourcing
- Brand is not trusted for specialist automotive advice
- Search experience is terrible for this use case

**How BuyingBuddy beats them:**
hipages is for finding a sparky. Not for buying a used car. Any overlap is incidental — but it's worth noting that buyers searching for "car inspection Brisbane" sometimes land here, which is a content gap BuyingBuddy should own.
