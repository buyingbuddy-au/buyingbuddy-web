### CONDITIONS OF SALE

1. **Deposit.** If a deposit is paid, it forms part of the purchase price. If the Buyer fails to complete the purchase without lawful reason, the Seller may retain the deposit as liquidated damages.

2. **Settlement.** The balance of the purchase price must be paid on or before the settlement date. Payment by bank transfer must be confirmed as cleared funds before the vehicle and keys are released.

3. **Transfer of Registration.** The Buyer is responsible for transferring the vehicle registration into their name within 14 days of the sale date, as required by the Transport Operations (Road Use Management—Vehicle Registration) Regulation 2021 (Qld).

4. **Safety Certificate.** The Seller must provide a current safety certificate (previously known as a roadworthy certificate) at or before the time of sale, unless the vehicle is sold as unregistered.

5. **Risk.** Risk in the vehicle passes to the Buyer upon settlement (i.e. when the full purchase price is paid and the vehicle and keys are handed over).

6. **No Warranty.** This is a private sale. No statutory warranty applies. The vehicle is sold in its current condition, subject only to the Seller's declarations above.

7. **PPSR.** The Buyer is encouraged to verify the vehicle's PPSR status immediately before settlement to confirm no new security interests have been registered since any prior search.

8. **Dispute Resolution.** In the event of a dispute, both parties agree to attempt to resolve the matter informally before commencing any legal proceedings.

### NOTES ON QLD PRIVATE SALES

**No cooling-off period:** Unlike dealer sales, private sales in Queensland have no mandatory cooling-off period. Once you pay and take the vehicle, the sale is final.

**No statutory warranty:** Private sellers are not required to provide any warranty. The vehicle is sold "as is" unless the seller has made specific representations about its condition.

**PPSR check is essential:** The Seller is not required to provide a PPSR certificate in a private sale. The Buyer should conduct their own PPSR search before paying. If the vehicle has money owing on it, the finance company can repossess it — even from the new owner.

**Safety certificate required for registration transfer:** A valid safety certificate (issued within 14 days prior to the application for transfer) is required to transfer registration in Queensland.

**Transfer duty:** The Buyer will be required to pay transfer duty (stamp duty) when transferring the registration. This is calculated as a percentage of the vehicle's value or purchase price.
